three months in Italy

How three months studying abroad helped strengthen my skills as a UX designer.


le storie dei italiani

On the second day that I arrived in Florence, I and the 34 other students were split up into groups, told to venture out into the city, strike up conversation with Italian strangers, and uncover/learn about their stories. Stories about what, you ask? Yeah, we weren't told specifics either. In my group of three, my grand ten weeks of Italian class were the only language skills we possessed. Confused and mildly terrified, we exited out of Palazzo Capponi and stepped onto the cobblestoned sidewalks. Luckily, we spotted something across the street that caught our attention. A couple taking pictures of wedding cakes resting on the ledge by Ponte Vecchio (an old stone bridge crossing the Arno River).

The next 180 seconds were filled with "wait we should talk to them!...I DON'T KNOW ITALIAN...hey you took Italian last quarter...YA TEN WEEKS...I know Spanish does that count...do we just approach them...this is gonna be awkward...dude idk what to say..." but somehow, we ended up walking across the street and striking up an initially disjointed and abrupt conversation.

What we didn't anticipate was that hesitancy melting into a storytelling journey. Of how he was born in Turkey and she was born in Korea and how they met at school in Italy. Of how she came to Italy to make leather handbags and how he came to Italy to be a chef. Of how his being a chef inspired her to begin baking cakes instead, and how now she is photographing wedding cakes on the famous bridge in Italy to upload on her website for her new cake baking business. Of how she's moving to Turkey with him for their wedding. It's not necessarily the story that amazed me, but moreso how people's lives could be so connected across distance. At that moment, I discovered how incredible it is to hear people's stories and imagine what it is like to live in their shoes and be inside their heads, and that ability to empathize, feeling what people feel and thinking what they think, is what I apply to my work in understanding people and their behaviors.
