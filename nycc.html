Mobile UX/UI Personal Project

This was a fun, side passion project I chose to work on my own time outside of work. I designed the workflow and prototype for a mobile app concept, which is neither part of a company nor a launched product.

Project Goal and Concept

Envision and design a mobile app for New York City Center. Show attendees search for upcoming shows and purchase tickets. User groups would span broad categories, from students on a budget to an older population of monthly donors and members.

Idea Conception

I first envisioned this idea while working at New York City Center as a call center representative. As a call center rep, I was responsible for selling show tickets over the phone, and the software I was using to find shows, available dates and times, complete the purchase, update their contact information, and send them a confirmation e-mail was laborious, manual, and unnecessary.

Two Main Types of Users

User Experience Issues

Design Didn't Match Human Behavior

The existing interface did not match how people would ask about shows and show times. First, people would say what show they wanted to see.

"I'd like tickets for 'Love Life'".

Then, they'd want to pick a specific date and time. Sometimes, they'd be vague.

"I'd like/Do you have tickets for Saturday mattinee (2 PM)."
Or "Anytime this weekend."

Call center reps had to scan every day of the week (sometimes scroll within a day) to locate the show time to find availability! This was a pain, and many customers became angry when we couldn't locate availability fast enough.

Simple Task and Prototype

I'm a student and I want to purchase one ticket for Love Life on March 10 at 4 PM. I'd like the ticket all the way on stage left in the front row on the balcony.
