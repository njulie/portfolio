New York City Center Mobile Design

This was a passion project I chose to work on after working briefly as a call center representative at New York City Center, Manhattan's oldest theater venue. I saw how painful it was for customers to purchase tickets online and personally helped customers buy tickets over the phone through the sales line. Wouldn't it be great if the theater had an app for customers to use?

Disclaimer: This is neither part of a company nor a launched product.

How did this project start?

I first envisioned this idea while working at New York City Center as a call center representative. As a call center rep, I was responsible for selling show tickets over the phone, and the software I was using to find shows, available dates and times, complete the purchase, update their contact information, and send them a confirmation e-mail was laborious, manual, and unnecessary.

Who'd be the app's primary users?

While I answered calls over the course of a month, I noticed incoming callers falling into two main groups.

Big issues with existing software

Existing design was painful

A key insight I gained on sales calls was that people generally knew what show they wanted to see.

They'd start the call by saying: "I'd like tickets for 'Love Life'".

Then, they'd want to pick a specific date and time.

"Do you have openings for Saturday mattinee (2 PM)?"

The existing calendar interface didn't allow sorting by show times, which made it painstaking to pick out specific show titles on each day.

Designing for the User

"I'm a student and I want to purchase one ticket for Love Life on March 10 at 4 PM. I'd like the ticket all the way on stage left in the front row on the balcony."
